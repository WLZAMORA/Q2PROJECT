PHILIPPINES

The Philippines is a Southeast Asian island nation situated in the western Pacific Ocean. Comprising over 7,000 islands and islets, it is located approximately 500 miles (800 km) off the shores of Vietnam. While Manila serves as the capital, Quezon City, situated nearby, is the most populous city and both are integral parts of the National Capital Region (Metro Manila), situated on Luzon, the largest island. The second largest island in the Philippines is Mindanao, positioned in the southeast.

SAGADA


Nestled in the mountain province of Luzon, Philippines, Sagada is a captivating town renowned for its scenic landscapes, distinctive burial traditions with hanging coffins, and a vibrant cultural heritage.

BAGUIO


Baguio, a city in west-central Luzon, Philippines, was proposed as the summer capital after the U.S. occupied the country in 1898. Nestled in pine-covered hills at an elevation of 4,900 feet, it became a premier resort with good connections to Manila. However, in 1976, Manila took over as the year-round capital, ending Baguio's official summer capital status.

BORACAY


Boracay Island, a premier destination among the best beaches in the Philippines and a top spot in the Visayas, boasts an extensive stretch of fine white sand known as White Beach, alongside clear azure waters and breathtaking sunsets. The island offers a plethora of adventures and activities suitable for individuals of all ages.
